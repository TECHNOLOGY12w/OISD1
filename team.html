Registration/Collaboration

The Oneworld Initiative for Sustainable Development (OISD) is fully recognized and registered with the CAC and a number of key government ministries, agencies, and institutions. These registrations affirm the organization’s credibility, legal standing, and accountability, ensuring compliance with statutory regulations while strengthening its ability to collaborate with both public and private stakeholders. Each registration serves as a foundation for implementing effective programs across diverse thematic areas, ranging from humanitarian response to education, women’s empowerment, and social development.Through these registrations, OISD is formally empowered to carry out its mandate across multiple sectors, building trust with communities, enhancing partnerships with government and development actors, and ensuring sustainability in all its operations.

Over the years, the Oneworld Initiative for Sustainable Development (OISD) has built an impressive track record of collaboration with government agencies, civil society organizations, development partners, and community-based initiatives. These partnerships have been instrumental in designing and implementing interventions that address conflict, promote peace, empower communities, and foster sustainable development. Through these collaborations, OISD has not only amplified its reach but also strengthened its credibility as a reliable partner in national and regional development. The synergy created with stakeholders has enabled the organization to scale its impact in areas such as human rights protection, peacebuilding, education, security, electoral integrity, and community development, OISD has demonstrated its ability to bridge the gap between policy and practice, ensuring that national strategies are translated into community-based impact. Each partnership underscores the organization’s core values of inclusivity, accountability, and sustainability.

Human Rights Commission:

Worked together on the promotion and protection of fundamental rights, with a focus on vulnerable and marginalized groups.

Legal Aid Council:

Provided legal support and mediation services for indigent persons and victims of abuse or conflict.

National Orientation Agency (NOA):

Partnered in community sensitization and public enlightenment campaigns aimed at fostering national unity and peace.

FCT Universal Basic Education Board (UBEB):

Collaborated to promote educational access, particularly for children affected by conflict and poverty.

Ministry of Information and Culture:

Implemented programs promoting cultural exchange and the use of cultural platforms as tools for peacebuilding.

National Drug Law Enforcement Agency (NDLEA):

Worked jointly on campaigns against substance abuse and its connection to crime and violence.

Grace Anthony Foundation:

Partnered on humanitarian and development initiatives targeting women and children.

Messenger of Peace Foundation:

Co-hosted peace advocacy events and grassroots conflict transformation programs.

National Independent Electoral Commission (INEC):

Supported awareness and advocacy for peaceful, transparent, and credible elections.

Small and Medium Enterprises Development Agency of Nigeria (SMEDAN):

Collaborated on youth and women empowerment programs through entrepreneurship and skills development.

Nigerian Television Authority (NTA 5):

Used television platforms for awareness campaigns and peace education programming.

Nigerian Security and Civil Defence Corps (NSCDC):

Partnered in security sensitization and community safety programs.

Abuja Municipal Area Council (AMAC):

Supported local development projects, conflict resolution, and community mobilization.

Save Our Future Today Initiative:

Partnered on child-focused programs aimed at protecting children’s rights and providing access to education and welfare services.

Area Council Service Secretariat:

Worked on grassroots interventions to improve local governance and service delivery.

Social Development Secretariat:

Provides OISD with the mandate to engage in social welfare and development initiatives that directly impact communities, particularly vulnerable groups.

Ministry of Budget and Planning:

Ensures OISD’s alignment with national development priorities, enabling the organization to design and execute programs that complement government planning frameworks.

FCT Social Development Secretariat:

Enhances OISD’s ability to deliver interventions across the Federal Capital Territory, with a focus on grassroots communities and marginalized populations.

Federal Ministry of Humanitarian Affairs:

Registration with this ministry affirms OISD’s role in humanitarian response, including disaster relief, social protection, and poverty alleviation programs.

Agency for Mass Education:

Strengthens OISD’s capacity to support literacy, vocational training, and lifelong learning programs, particularly for out-of-school children, women, and disadvantaged youth.

Ministry of Women Affairs:

Provides OISD with a platform to advance programs on gender equality, women empowerment, girl-child education, and protection of women’s rights.

Ministry of Education:

Aligns OISD’s educational programs with national policy frameworks, supporting access to quality education and sustainable learning opportunities across communities.

Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC):

Registration with the EFCC strengthens OISD’s institutional accountability, transparency, and compliance with anti-corruption regulations, positioning the organization as a trustworthy partner in national development.
